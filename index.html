Anthropic’s Mythos goes public: key details explained

The early release of Mythos signals a shift in Anthropic’s strategy, with implications for how AI tools are built and used

📷 Anthropic’s decision to release Mythos ahead of schedule has caught the AI industry off guard, with rivals scrambling to respond. Photograph: Samuel Boivin/NurPhoto/Shutterstock

In a move that surprised much of the technology industry, Anthropic announced on Friday that it would make Mythos — its most powerful AI model to date — available to the public, reversing months of internal debate about whether the system was safe enough for widespread deployment.

The decision marks a sharp departure from the San Francisco company’s traditionally cautious approach to releasing frontier AI systems. Anthropic had previously signalled that Mythos would remain restricted to a small number of government and enterprise partners for at least another six months.

“We’ve reached a point where we believe the benefits of broad access outweigh the risks of restricted deployment,” said Dario Amodei, Anthropic’s chief executive, in a statement. “The safety evaluations gave us confidence that responsible release was the right path.”

Industry analysts say the early release was driven in part by competitive pressure from OpenAI and Google DeepMind, both of which have announced plans to ship models with comparable capabilities in the coming weeks. Anthropic’s hand may have been forced by the prospect of losing its lead in what has become an increasingly crowded market.

The UK’s AI Safety Institute confirmed it had conducted preliminary evaluations of Mythos but declined to comment on whether it had formally signed off on the public release. A spokesperson said only that “discussions with Anthropic are ongoing”.
